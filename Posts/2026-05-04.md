## Full Post
43% of meeting room tech issues go unreported.

Users don't log a ticket.
They just switch to their laptop.
And book the room again tomorrow.

Most IT teams don't have a Teams Rooms problem.
They have a visibility problem.

They don't know which rooms are unhealthy.
They don't know which rooms nobody actually uses.
They don't know that three rooms haven't had a firmware update in four months.

Until someone important has a bad meeting.
Then it becomes urgent.

Here's what I see in mid-market organisations all the time:

 - Room listed in Teams Admin Center as "healthy" — but camera defaulting to wrong input
 - Booking rate at 90% — utilisation rate at 40% (ghost meetings)
 - No automated alerts, so IT only finds out when a user complains
 - Room accounts with no conditional access, exposed to misuse
