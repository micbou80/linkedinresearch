# The hidden cost of unmanaged Microsoft Teams Rooms — and how to fix it in one afternoon

## Hook
42% of meeting rooms have an active problem right now.

## Full Post
42% of meeting rooms have an active problem right now.

A dead camera. A firmware version behind. A room account about to lock out at 2am.

Most IT teams don't have a Microsoft Teams Rooms problem.
They have a visibility problem.

They bought the hardware. Deployed the rooms. Moved on.
And now those rooms are quietly failing — one bad meeting at a time.

Here's what I see most often:

 - Firmware 2–3 versions behind (blocks new features, carries known bugs)
 - Resource account passwords set to expire (rooms go dark overnight)
 - No-one-touch join for external platforms (hybrid meeting falls apart in front of the client)
 - MFA misconfigured on room accounts (lockout happens at the worst moment)

None of these are hard to fix.
All of them are easy to miss when you have 20+ rooms and no structured audit process.

I put together a free checklist: 15 checks, 15 minutes per room, clear scoring at the end.

It covers device health, audio/video quality, meeting experience, and security — with a quick-fix reference card for the most common failures.

If you manage Teams Rooms — or sell them — this gives you a defensible baseline in one afternoon.
