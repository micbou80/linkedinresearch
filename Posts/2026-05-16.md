# The hidden cost of bad hybrid meeting rooms — and how IT teams can fix it fast

## Hook
Your meeting room passed IT sign-off. Your remote colleagues hate it anyway.

## Full Post
Your meeting room passed IT sign-off. Your remote colleagues hate it anyway.

Here's the gap nobody talks about:

Most IT teams validate a room at go-live.
Camera? On. Audio? Works. Screen? Displays.

Then they move on.

But "works" in a room walkthrough is not the same as "works for the person dialling in from home."

The remote participant is fighting:
 - Echo from a ceiling speaker no one repositioned
 - A camera that frames the whiteboard instead of the faces
 - A screenshare in 10pt font they can't read
 - A mic that drops the person at the far end of the table

None of these show up in your ticket queue.
They show up in meeting fatigue, checked-out remote staff, and decisions made by the people in the room.

The fix isn't always expensive.
Sometimes it's enabling IntelliFrame.
Sometimes it's a €200 table mic.
Sometimes it's just moving a chair out of the camera's blind spot.

But you can't fix what you haven't audited.

I put together a 10-point hybrid room audit checklist — takes under 15 minutes per room, tells you exactly what's failing and what to fix first.
