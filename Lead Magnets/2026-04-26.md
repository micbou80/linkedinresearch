## Step 3 — Calculate Your Idle Room Cost

```
Idle cost per month =
  Total monthly cost × (1 - your utilisation rate)
```

**Example:**
- 12 rooms × $420/month average = $5,040/month total
- Utilisation: 38%
- Idle cost: $5,040 × 0.62 = **$3,125/month** sitting unused
- Annualised: **$37,500/year**

## Step 4 — The Meeting Friction Tax

Every failed room start costs time. Research puts average troubleshooting time at **6.3 minutes per incident**.

```
Friction tax per month =
  # of meetings/month × failure rate % × 6.3 min × avg. attendee hourly rate
```

**Inputs:**
- Estimated meetings per month across all rooms: ______
- % that have a tech issue at start (be honest — industry avg: 23%): ____%
- Average attendee fully-loaded hourly rate: $______
- Average attendees per meeting: ______

**Example:**
- 400 meetings/month × 23% × 0.105 hrs × $85/hr × 5 attendees
- = **$4,085/month in lost productivity**
- Annualised: **$49,020/year**

## The Fix: 3 Actions That Move Utilisation from 38% to 60%+

**Action 1 — Enable hot room booking via Teams panels**
Rooms without a visible, frictionless booking panel get skipped. Teams Rooms panels (e.g. Logitech Tap, Crestron TSS) cut booking friction by ~40%.

**Action 2 — Turn on Room Finder in Outlook and Teams**
Less than 30% of orgs have this configured correctly. It takes 2 hours to set up. It surfaces the right room automatically based on attendee count and location.

**Action 3 — Set a monthly utilisation review cadence**
Teams Admin Center > Reports > Device Usage. One person. 20 minutes. Monthly. Rooms below 30% get a "why" investigation before next cycle.
