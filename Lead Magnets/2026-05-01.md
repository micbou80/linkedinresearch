# The Teams Rooms Health Audit Checklist: 15 Checks in 15 Minutes

# The Teams Rooms Health Audit Checklist
## 15 Checks in 15 Minutes
*For IT Managers, MSPs, and AV Integrators managing Microsoft Teams Rooms*

---

### Why This Matters
A single broken meeting room costs your organisation an average of **$1,200/month** in lost productivity (Forrester, 2024 hybrid work study). Most IT teams only discover problems *after* a VP complains. This checklist flips that.

Run it monthly. Takes 15 minutes. Prevents the 3am Slack message.

---

### Section 1: Device Health (5 min)

- [ ] **All rooms visible in Teams Admin Center** — Log in to admin.teams.microsoft.com → Devices → Teams Rooms. Every room should show as Online. Flag anything Offline or Unknown.
- [ ] **No rooms showing 'Needs attention' signal** — Check the health dashboard. Red/yellow = act today, not next sprint.
- [ ] **Firmware is current on compute units** — Filter by device type, sort by firmware version. Anything 2+ versions behind is a risk.
- [ ] **Camera and audio peripherals recognised** — Click into each room. Confirm peripheral status shows 'Connected', not 'Not detected'.
- [ ] **App version is within 1 release of current** — Teams Rooms app updates ship monthly. Rooms running N-2 or older miss bug fixes and new AI features (e.g., Intelligent Speaker, Copilot in Teams Rooms).

**Red flag threshold:** More than 2 devices offline = systematic problem (licensing, network, or update policy). Escalate immediately.

---

### Section 2: Meeting Room Experience (4 min)

- [ ] **Test one-touch join works in each room** — Book a test meeting. Walk in 2 minutes before. The join button should appear automatically on the console.
- [ ] **Content sharing tested (HDMI + wireless)** — Plug in a laptop. Confirm content appears on the room display within 5 seconds. Test wireless share (Miracast / Teams Cast) if enabled.
- [ ] **Camera framing is calibrated** — Join a test call. Confirm the camera auto-frames to seated participants. If using intelligent camera (e.g., Jabra PanaCast, Poly Studio), verify active speaker tracking is on.
- [ ] **Room audio passes the 'back row test'** — Sit at the far end of the table. Speak at normal volume. Confirm remote participants can hear clearly (no echo, no clipping).
- [ ] **Dual-display rooms show roster + content correctly** — If the room has two screens, confirm one shows video and one shows shared content by default.

**Quick fix:** Most one-touch join failures trace to calendar sync issues. Check the resource account in Microsoft 365 Admin → confirm AutoAccept is on and mailbox is licensed.

---

### Section 3: Security & Compliance (3 min)

- [ ] **Resource accounts use dedicated service accounts** — Not a named user's account. Named users leave. Service accounts don't.
- [ ] **MFA exemption is policy-documented, not ad hoc** — Teams Rooms compute units need MFA excluded from Conditional Access. Confirm this is in a named, reviewed CA policy — not a forgotten legacy exclusion.
- [ ] **Rooms are enrolled in Intune (or MDM equivalent)** — Admin Center → Devices → confirm management state. Unmanaged rooms are a compliance gap.
- [ ] **Remote restart capability confirmed** — Can you restart a room compute unit without physically entering the room? If no: set up Teams Rooms Pro Management or equivalent remote access now.

---

### Section 4: AI & Copilot Readiness (3 min)

- [ ] **Teams Rooms Pro license assigned (not Teams Rooms Basic)** — Pro unlocks AI features: intelligent audio, meeting room analytics, Copilot in Teams Rooms. Basic is free but limited. Check: Admin Center → Billing → Licenses.
- [ ] **Intelligent Speaker configured for speaker attribution** — If you want Copilot transcripts to label who said what, Intelligent Speaker must be enabled AND participants must have their voice profiles enrolled. Check: Admin Center → Teams Rooms → Configuration.

---

### Scoring Your Audit

| Checks Passed | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 13–15 | ✅ Healthy | Schedule next audit in 30 days |
| 9–12 | ⚠️ At Risk | Fix red flags this week |
| 0–8 | 🔴 Critical | Escalate. Users are already experiencing failures. |

### Bonus: The 3 Fixes That Solve 80% of Teams Rooms Issues

1. **Restart schedule** — Set a nightly auto-restart (2am) via Teams Rooms Pro Management or a GPO restart task. Clears update hangs and memory leaks.
2. **Calendar sync check** — 'Meeting not showing on console' is almost always a resource account permission issue. Run: `Get-CalendarProcessing -Identity room@yourdomain.com` in Exchange Online PowerShell. AutomateProcessing should be AutoAccept.
3. **Network QoS tagging** — Teams audio/video traffic should be tagged DSCP 46 (Expedited Forwarding). Untagged rooms suffer quality issues that no firmware update will fix.
