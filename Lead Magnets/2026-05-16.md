# The Hybrid Meeting Room Audit Checklist: 10 Checks That Reveal Why Your Rooms Are Failing (and What to Fix First)

# The Hybrid Meeting Room Audit Checklist
## 10 Checks That Reveal Why Your Rooms Are Failing — and What to Fix First

**Who this is for:** IT Managers, IT Directors, and AV Integrators responsible for Microsoft Teams Rooms deployments at mid-market companies.

**Time to complete:** Under 15 minutes per room.

**What you get:** A clear picture of where your rooms are failing remote participants — and a prioritised fix list.

---

## Before You Start

Grab a laptop, a remote colleague on Teams, and walk through each room. Score each item: ✅ Pass / ⚠️ Partial / ❌ Fail.

---

## Section 1: Audio (Biggest impact on remote experience)

- [ ] **1. Voice pickup at the far end of the table**
  Remote participant can clearly hear someone speaking from the seat furthest from the mic.
  *Fix if failing: Add a Teams-certified table mic (e.g. Yealink CPW90, Sennheiser TeamConnect) or upgrade to a beamforming ceiling mic for rooms >6 people.*

- [ ] **2. Echo and reverb check**
  Remote participant hears no echo of their own voice back at them.
  *Fix if failing: Enable Microsoft Teams Rooms echo cancellation in device settings. Check room acoustics — hard surfaces need soft furnishings or acoustic panels.*

- [ ] **3. Background noise suppression**
  HVAC, keyboard, and chair noise is suppressed for remote participants.
  *Fix if failing: Enable "High" noise suppression in Teams admin center under Meeting Policies → Audio.*

---

## Section 2: Camera and Video

- [ ] **4. Active speaker tracking**
  Camera follows the active speaker automatically without manual PTZ adjustment.
  *Fix if failing: Enable IntelliFrame or Speaker Tracking in your Teams Rooms Pro Management portal. Confirm device supports AI framing (e.g. Logitech Rally Bar, Poly Studio).*

- [ ] **5. Remote participant can see all in-room attendees**
  No one is sitting outside the camera's field of view.
  *Fix if failing: Reposition camera or switch to a wider FOV lens. For rooms >8 people, consider dual-camera setup.*

- [ ] **6. Lighting adequacy**
  Faces are clearly lit — no backlit silhouettes from windows behind participants.
  *Fix if failing: Add a bias light behind the screen or reposition seating. This is the cheapest fix with the highest visual impact.*

---

## Section 3: Display and Content Sharing

- [ ] **7. Content is legible on remote call**
  Screenshared content (slides, spreadsheets) is readable without zooming on a standard 1080p remote display.
  *Fix if failing: Set minimum font size policy for presentations (recommend 24pt minimum). Enable Teams content optimisation mode.*

- [ ] **8. Front Row layout is enabled (where applicable)**
  Dual-display rooms are running Teams Rooms Front Row to show remote faces at eye level on the lower screen.
  *Fix if failing: Enable Front Row in Teams Rooms settings. Requires Teams Rooms Pro licence.*

---

## Section 4: Device Health and Management

- [ ] **9. Device is enrolled in Teams Rooms Pro Management**
  Room device appears in the Pro Management portal with health status Green.
  *Fix if failing: Enrol device at aka.ms/mtrp. Pro Management gives you real-time alerts, remote restart, and AI-generated room quality signals.*

- [ ] **10. Firmware and app are current**
  Teams Rooms app and device firmware are within one version of the current release.
  *Fix if failing: Set auto-update policy in Teams admin center → Teams Rooms on Windows → Update policies. Stale firmware is the #1 cause of unexplained call quality drops.*

---

## Your Audit Scorecard

| Score | What it means | Priority action |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| 9–10 ✅ | Room is performing well | Schedule quarterly re-audit |
| 6–8 ✅ | Moderate issues — remote experience is degraded | Fix audio items first (highest impact) |
| 3–5 ✅ | Significant problems — remote participants are second-class | Escalate: budget case for hardware refresh |
| 0–2 ✅ | Room is broken for hybrid | Take out of service until fixed |

---

## The One Fix That Pays Back Fastest

If you only fix one thing: **sort the audio first.**

Research from Microsoft's Modern Work team consistently shows that poor audio — not video — is the #1 reason people disengage from hybrid calls. A €200 table mic upgrade in a €50,000 meeting room is the highest-ROI IT intervention most companies aren't making.
