# The Teams Rooms Health Audit: 10-Point Checklist to Find (and Fix) Silent Meeting Failures

# The Teams Rooms Health Audit
## 10-Point Checklist to Find (and Fix) Silent Meeting Failures

**Who this is for:** IT Managers and AV/MSP teams managing Microsoft Teams Rooms at mid-market organisations.

**Time to complete:** 15 minutes per room. Run it once, then automate the flags.

**What you'll catch:** The specific failures that erode hybrid meeting quality — and meeting trust — without ever showing up in a helpdesk ticket.

---

### Before You Start

Open the **Teams Rooms Pro Management portal** (or Teams Admin Center for Basic) in one tab. Have physical access to at least one room, or a remote session open.

Score each item: ✅ Pass | ⚠️ Risk | ❌ Fail

---

### Section 1: Device Health (the stuff that fails silently)

**1. Firmware is current**
- Check: Teams Admin Center → Devices → Teams Rooms → select device → Device details → Software versions
- Target: Within 1 major version of current release
- Why it matters: Outdated firmware is the #1 cause of unexplained call drops and camera freeze
- Fix time if failing: 10 minutes (schedule overnight update push)
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**2. Device heartbeat is healthy (last seen < 5 minutes ago)**
- Check: Teams Admin Center → Devices → Teams Rooms → "Last seen" column
- Target: < 5 min. Anything over 15 min = offline risk
- Why it matters: A device that hasn't checked in is a device that will fail at 9am Monday
- Fix time if failing: 5 minutes (power cycle + network check)
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**3. No active critical alerts in Pro Management**
- Check: Teams Rooms Pro Management → Incidents → filter by severity: Critical/Important
- Target: Zero unacknowledged critical alerts
- Why it matters: Pro Management flags hardware faults up to 48 hours before user complaints arrive
- Fix time if failing: Depends on alert type — triage takes 5 min
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

---

### Section 2: Audio/Video Quality (what remote participants actually experience)

**4. Default microphone is the room mic — not a laptop or USB fallback**
- Check: On the Teams Rooms console → Settings → Device settings → Microphone
- Target: Room mic (e.g. Jabra, Poly, Shure, Sennheiser) set as default — NOT "Microsoft Teams Audio"
- Why it matters: When someone plugs in a laptop, Teams can silently switch to laptop mic. Remote participants hear hollow, echo-filled audio.
- Fix time if failing: 2 minutes (lock default in XML config)
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**5. Camera framing is set to Auto-framing (Intelligent Speaker where licensed)**
- Check: Console → Settings → Camera → verify auto-framing is ON
- Target: Enabled. For rooms > 6 people: Intelligent Speaker licence applied
- Why it matters: Static wide-angle cameras make remote participants feel excluded. This is the single biggest equity gap in hybrid meetings.
- Fix time if failing: 3 minutes (toggle + verify)
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**6. Audio test passes in the last 7 days (Pro Management signal)**
- Check: Pro Management → Device → Signal history → Audio peripherals
- Target: Green signal, no degradation flags
- Why it matters: Gradual mic degradation is invisible until a VP complains
- Fix time if failing: Schedule manual test + physical inspection
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

---

### Section 3: Meeting Room Experience (what in-room users experience)

**7. One-touch join is working for the next scheduled meeting**
- Check: Walk into the room 10 minutes before any scheduled meeting. The meeting tile should appear on the console automatically.
- Target: Tile visible with correct meeting title and organiser
- Why it matters: If one-touch join is broken, users fall back to BYOD — killing room analytics and audio quality
- Fix time if failing: Check room mailbox calendar sync + Exchange/Outlook permissions (30 min fix)
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**8. Display output is correct (no black screens, no "No Signal" fallback)**
- Check: Physically verify both displays (if dual-screen room) show the Teams Rooms home screen
- Target: Both displays active, no HDMI handshake errors
- Why it matters: A black second screen means content sharing is broken — and no one reports it until a client presentation
- Fix time if failing: HDMI cable swap or display setting check — 10 minutes
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

---

### Section 4: Licence and Configuration Hygiene

**9. Room account has correct licence assigned (Teams Rooms Basic or Pro — not a user licence)**
- Check: Microsoft 365 Admin Center → Users → [room account] → Licences
- Target: Teams Rooms Basic (free, up to 25 rooms) or Teams Rooms Pro. NOT Teams Essentials or Microsoft 365 Business Standard.
- Why it matters: User licences on room accounts cause sign-in loops, missing features, and compliance gaps
- Fix time if failing: Licence swap — 5 minutes. May require room console reboot.
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

**10. Room is enrolled in Teams Rooms Pro Management (even on Basic rooms)**
- Check: Teams Rooms Pro Management portal → Rooms inventory
- Target: All rooms visible, no "unenrolled" status
- Why it matters: Without Pro Management, you're flying blind. You find out about failures from angry emails, not dashboards.
- Fix time if failing: Deploy monitoring agent — 15 minutes per room
- Score: ✅ ⚠️ ❌

---

### Your Score

| Score | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| 10/10 ✅ | Your rooms are genuinely well-managed. Document it and use it as a baseline. |
| 7–9 ✅ | Good foundation. Fix the ⚠️ risks before they become ❌ failures. |
| 4–6 mix | You have silent failures happening right now. Prioritise Section 1 and 4 first. |
| 0–3 ✅ | Your meeting rooms are costing you money and credibility. Start with items 1, 2, 9, 10. |

---

### Quick-Fix Priority Order (if you have 1 hour today)

1. Fix licence misassignments (item 9) — highest risk, easiest fix
2. Push firmware updates (item 1) — schedule for tonight
3. Enrol all rooms in Pro Management (item 10) — gives you visibility on everything else
4. Lock default microphone config (item 4) — prevents the silent audio degradation no one reports
