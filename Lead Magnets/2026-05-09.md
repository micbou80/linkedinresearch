# The Teams Rooms Health Checklist: 15 Checks Every IT Manager Should Run Before the Next All-Hands

# The Teams Rooms Health Checklist
## 15 Checks Every IT Manager Should Run Before the Next All-Hands

*Time required: ~45 minutes | Works for: IT Managers, AV Integrators, MSPs*

---

### Why this exists

The average enterprise pays €8,000–€25,000 per Teams Room device deployment. Yet most rooms sit unmonitored until someone complains in the all-hands meeting — in front of 300 people.

This checklist prevents that.

Run it monthly. Delegate it to a junior tech. Track your score over time.

---

## SECTION 1: Device Health (5 checks)

- [ ] **1. All devices are online in Teams Admin Center**
  - Go to: Teams Admin Center → Devices → Teams Rooms
  - Flag any device showing "Offline" or "Unhealthy"
  - Target: 100% online 24h before the meeting

- [ ] **2. App version is current (within 1 major release)**
  - Acceptable lag: 1 version behind
  - Red flag: 2+ versions behind (security + feature risk)
  - Where to check: Teams Admin Center → device detail → App version

- [ ] **3. Windows OS is patched within the last 60 days**
  - Unpatched devices = compliance and stability risk
  - Note the patch date in your asset register

- [ ] **4. Peripheral firmware is up to date (camera, speaker, compute bar)**
  - Logitech, Poly, Yealink, Neat — each has its own firmware portal
  - Tip: Set a calendar reminder to check vendor portals monthly

- [ ] **5. Room resource account has a valid, non-expired password / license**
  - A lapsed M365 license silently breaks booking and call functionality
  - Check: Microsoft 365 Admin Center → Users → [Room Account] → Licenses

---

## SECTION 2: Audio & Video Quality (4 checks)

- [ ] **6. Camera field of view covers all seats in the room**
  - Test: sit at the far corner — are you visible?
  - Flag rooms where corner seats are cut off

- [ ] **7. Microphone pickup reaches the furthest seat**
  - Test with a colleague: speak from 4m away in a normal voice
  - Remote participant confirms they can hear clearly without echo

- [ ] **8. No persistent echo or feedback reported in last 30 days**
  - Check: Teams Admin Center → Call Quality Dashboard → filter by room
  - Or: ask your service desk for tickets tagged with room name

- [ ] **9. Speaker volume is calibrated — not maxed out, not too low**
  - Default max causes distortion; default low causes complaints
  - Set to 70–80% and test with a live call

---

## SECTION 3: Booking & Scheduling (3 checks)

- [ ] **10. Room appears correctly in Outlook/Teams calendar**
  - Book a test meeting — confirm it shows as accepted and appears on the room display
  - Common failure: resource mailbox auto-accept is turned off

- [ ] **11. Room display shows the correct upcoming schedule**
  - Walk up to the panel — does it show today's bookings?
  - Blank or frozen display = firmware or network issue

- [ ] **12. "One-touch join" works for a scheduled Teams meeting**
  - The join button should appear 5 minutes before the meeting
  - If it doesn't: check calendar processing rules on the resource account

---

## SECTION 4: Network & Reliability (3 checks)

- [ ] **13. Device is on a dedicated VLAN or QoS-tagged network segment**
  - AV traffic competing with general office traffic = dropped calls
  - If not segmented: flag this as a priority infrastructure task

- [ ] **14. Bandwidth to room device meets Microsoft minimums**
  - Minimum: 4 Mbps up/down per room for 1080p video
  - Test: run a speed test from the compute unit (remote desktop in)

- [ ] **15. Device has been restarted in the last 7 days**
  - Stale sessions cause ghost bugs (frozen UI, audio dropouts)
  - Best practice: schedule a nightly restart via Teams Admin Center or a PowerShell script

---

## Scoring Your Rooms

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 13–15 ✅ | Healthy | Monitor monthly |
| 9–12 ⚠️ | At Risk | Schedule remediation within 2 weeks |
| 0–8 🔴 | Critical | Do not use for exec/all-hands meetings |

---

## Quick Fix: The 3 Issues That Cause 80% of Complaints

1. **Expired room account license** — fix in 2 minutes in M365 Admin Center
2. **Outdated Teams Rooms app** — push update via Teams Admin Center → device → Update
3. **No nightly restart** — schedule via: Teams Admin Center → Configuration Profiles → Device restart schedule

## Bonus: PowerShell One-Liner to List All Room Device Statuses

```powershell
# Requires MicrosoftTeams PowerShell module
Get-CsOnlineDevice | Where-Object {$_.DeviceType -eq "TeamsRoom"} | Select-Object DisplayName, HealthStatus, CurrentVersion | Export-Csv .\TeamsRooms_Health.csv -NoTypeInformation
```

Export this CSV, sort by HealthStatus, and you have your remediation list in 60 seconds.
