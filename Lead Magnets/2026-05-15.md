# The Teams Rooms Health Audit Checklist: 15 Checks in 15 Minutes

# The Teams Rooms Health Audit Checklist
## 15 Checks in 15 Minutes
*For IT Managers, AV Integrators & MSPs | Michel Bouman | May 2026*

---

### Why this checklist exists

The average mid-market company has 12–40 meeting rooms equipped with Microsoft Teams Rooms devices. Studies show **42% of those rooms have at least one unresolved issue at any given time** — a dead camera, a misconfigured audio setting, a firmware version that blocks a new feature.

Each broken room costs roughly **$150–$400 per failed meeting** in lost productivity (average 6 attendees × 45 min × blended hourly rate). Do the math for your estate.

This checklist takes 15 minutes per room and gives you a clear picture of what's broken, what's at risk, and what to fix first.

---

### Before You Start

- [ ] Open the **Microsoft Teams Admin Center** (admin.teams.microsoft.com) in one browser tab
- [ ] Open **Teams Rooms Pro Management portal** (if licensed) in a second tab
- [ ] Have the room's device name and IP address to hand
- [ ] Run this audit during off-peak hours (avoid live meetings)

---

### Section 1: Device Health (5 checks)

- [ ] **1. Firmware is current**
  - Go to: Teams Admin Center → Devices → Teams Rooms on Windows/Android
  - Flag any device more than 2 versions behind current release
  - *Risk if skipped: known audio/video bugs, security vulnerabilities*

- [ ] **2. App version matches recommended baseline**
  - Microsoft publishes a recommended app version monthly
  - Check: [aka.ms/teamsroomsrelease](https://aka.ms/teamsroomsrelease)
  - *Risk if skipped: feature gaps, join-meeting failures*

- [ ] **3. Device is showing "Healthy" in Admin Center**
  - Any status other than Healthy = investigate before the next meeting
  - Common culprits: certificate expiry, network timeout, sign-in loop

- [ ] **4. Last heartbeat was within 24 hours**
  - A device that hasn't checked in is either offline or stuck
  - *Quick fix: remote restart via Admin Center or Pro Management portal*

- [ ] **5. No active alerts in Teams Rooms Pro Management**
  - Review alert queue; dismiss resolved, assign open items with a due date

---

### Section 2: Audio & Video Quality (4 checks)

- [ ] **6. Camera is detected and set as default**
  - Walk into the room and confirm the camera preview shows on the console
  - In device settings: confirm correct camera selected as default

- [ ] **7. Microphone picks up from all seats**
  - Speak from the far corner of the room — does the console show audio input?
  - For rooms > 6 people: verify ceiling mic or satellite mic is active

- [ ] **8. Speaker volume is calibrated (not at default)**
  - Default volume is often too low for larger rooms or too high for huddle spaces
  - Target: conversational level at 60% volume

- [ ] **9. Noise suppression is enabled**
  - Teams Rooms settings → Audio → confirm AI noise suppression is ON
  - *Especially critical for rooms near open-plan areas or kitchens*

---

### Section 3: Meeting Experience (3 checks)

- [ ] **10. One-touch join works for Teams AND (if applicable) Zoom/Google Meet**
  - Book a test meeting and walk up to the console — does it appear on screen?
  - Direct Guest Join must be enabled in Admin Center if using third-party platforms

- [ ] **11. Content sharing (HDMI/wireless) is functional**
  - Plug in a laptop via the room's cable — does it share to the display within 5 seconds?
  - Test wireless sharing (Miracast / Teams Cast) from a mobile device

- [ ] **12. Front Row or Gallery view is configured correctly**
  - For hybrid-first rooms: enable Front Row layout as default
  - Confirm the layout actually renders correctly on the room display

---

### Section 4: Security & Compliance (3 checks)

- [ ] **13. Room account has MFA configured correctly**
  - Room accounts need MFA *excluded* from Conditional Access (use a dedicated CA policy)
  - Misconfigured MFA = room gets locked out at 2am before a 9am all-hands

- [ ] **14. Resource account password is set to never expire (or managed via service)**
  - Password expiry on room accounts is the #1 cause of overnight outages
  - Fix: Set-MsolUser -UserPrincipalName room@domain.com -PasswordNeverExpires $true

- [ ] **15. Device is enrolled in Intune (if applicable)**
  - Compliance policy should enforce disk encryption and OS version
  - Unenrolled devices are invisible to your security team

---

### Scoring Your Audit

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 14–15 checks passed | ✅ Healthy | Schedule next audit in 30 days |
| 10–13 checks passed | ⚠️ At Risk | Fix flagged items within 1 week |
| Below 10 checks passed | 🔴 Critical | Escalate; room should be taken offline until resolved |

---

### Quick Fix Reference Card

| Problem | Fix | Time |
|---------|-----|------|
| Firmware out of date | Admin Center → Update → Schedule overnight | 5 min to schedule |
| Device offline | Remote restart via Admin Center | 2 min |
| Password expiry | Set PasswordNeverExpires via PowerShell | 3 min |
| MFA lockout | Exclude room account from CA policy | 10 min |
| Camera not detected | Reboot device; check USB hub power | 5 min |
| No one-touch join | Enable Direct Guest Join in Admin Center | 5 min |

---

### What to Do With Your Results

1. **Export** your audit results to a simple spreadsheet (Room name | Check | Pass/Fail | Owner | Due Date)
2. **Prioritise** security checks first (items 13–15), then meeting experience (10–12)
3. **Schedule** a monthly audit cadence — block 2 hours in your calendar now
4. **Report** your estate health score to leadership: "X% of our rooms are meeting-ready today"
