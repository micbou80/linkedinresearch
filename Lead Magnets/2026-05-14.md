# The Teams Rooms Health Checklist: 15 Checks in 15 Minutes

# The Teams Rooms Health Checklist
## 15 Checks in 15 Minutes — For IT Managers Who Can't Afford a Bad Meeting

> Run this checklist once a month. Each item takes under 60 seconds. Catch problems before your CFO walks into a broken room.

---

### SECTION 1: Device Health (5 min)

- [ ] **1. Check Teams Admin Center device status**
  Open TAC → Devices → Teams Rooms. Every room should show 'Healthy'. Flag anything showing 'Unhealthy' or 'Offline'.
  *Target: 0 unhealthy devices.*

- [ ] **2. Confirm app version is current**
  TAC → Devices → select room → check Teams Rooms app version. If more than one version behind, schedule update.
  *Current stable baseline: check aka.ms/teamsroomsrelease*

- [ ] **3. Verify Windows OS update status**
  TAC or Intune → confirm OS patches applied within last 30 days.

- [ ] **4. Check peripheral connectivity**
  Camera, microphone, speaker all showing as connected in TAC device detail. No 'peripheral offline' alerts.

- [ ] **5. Confirm compute unit is online and reachable**
  Ping or remote into the device if needed. Confirm it hasn't been powered off manually.

---

### SECTION 2: Meeting Experience (4 min)

- [ ] **6. Test one-touch join**
  Walk into a room. Tap 'Join' on an existing calendar meeting. Should connect in under 10 seconds.

- [ ] **7. Verify calendar sync**
  Room calendar should show today's bookings on the console. If blank or stale, check Exchange/M365 room mailbox sync.

- [ ] **8. Audio check — local echo test**
  Join a test meeting. Clap once loudly. Confirm no echo or feedback loop in the room.

- [ ] **9. Camera framing check**
  Confirm camera is centred on the room, not the ceiling or a wall. AI framing (if enabled) should auto-track correctly.

- [ ] **10. Content sharing test**
  Plug in or wirelessly share a laptop screen. Confirm it appears within 5 seconds on the room display.

---

### SECTION 3: Configuration & Security (4 min)

- [ ] **11. Confirm room account is licensed correctly**
  M365 Admin Center → Users → room account. Should hold a Teams Rooms Pro or Teams Rooms Basic licence. No expired licences.

- [ ] **12. Check sign-in status**
  TAC → device detail → confirm room account is signed in. 'Signed out' rooms can't join meetings.

- [ ] **13. Verify conditional access / MFA isn't blocking the room account**
  Room accounts must be excluded from MFA policies. Confirm exclusion group still includes this account.

- [ ] **14. Review alert rules in TAC**
  TAC → Notifications & Alerts → confirm at least one alert rule is active for offline devices. Silent rooms = invisible failures.

- [ ] **15. Check Pro Management Portal (if licensed)**
  If using Teams Rooms Pro, review the incident queue. Any open incidents older than 7 days? Assign and resolve.

---

### SCORING

| Checks Passed | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 14–15 | ✅ Healthy | Schedule next review in 30 days |
| 11–13 | ⚠️ At Risk | Fix flagged items this week |
| ≤10 | 🔴 Critical | Escalate — rooms will fail in meetings |

---

### BONUS: The One Metric to Track Monthly

**Room Utilisation Rate** = Meetings held in room ÷ Meetings booked in room × 100

If this drops below 70%, the room experience is likely failing users — even if TAC shows 'Healthy'.

Pull this from: M365 Admin Center → Reports → Microsoft Teams → Teams Rooms usage.
